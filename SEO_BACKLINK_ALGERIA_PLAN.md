# Adantimer Backlink Plan: Algeria FR/AR Focus

## Objective

Build a small number of relevant, clean backlinks that support the Algeria cluster first.

Priority pages:

- `/fr/oran`
- `/ar/oran`
- `/fr/alger`
- `/ar/alger`
- `/fr/annaba`
- `/ar/annaba`
- `/fr/bouira`
- `/ar/bouira`
- `/fr/ain-benian`
- `/ar/ain-benian`

Secondary supporting pages:

- `/fr/prayer-times`
- `/fr/next-prayer`
- `/fr/fajr-time`
- `/fr/maghrib-time`
- `/fr/isha-time`
- `/ar/prayer-times`
- `/ar/next-prayer`

## Rules

- No paid spam links.
- No comment spam.
- No AI guest-post farms.
- No sitewide footer link schemes.
- No exact-match anchor repetition across every placement.

The right target is a real community or religious resource page that would still make sense even if Google did not exist.

## What Counts As A Good Link

A good backlink for this phase is:

- topically relevant to Islam, prayer times, mosque life, or Muslim community services
- in French or Arabic, or clearly used by Algerian or North African audiences
- placed in real page content, a community resources section, or a useful-tools page
- on a live site with visible maintenance, real contact details, and non-spam content

## Priority Target Types

### 1. Mosque and community websites

Best fit for this phase.

Why:

- strongest topical relevance
- users already care about prayer times
- useful-links or community-tools sections are common

Best outreach angle:

- "This is a fast city-based prayer time and next-prayer lookup tool for Algeria in French and Arabic."

### 2. Muslim associations in France with North African audiences

Best fit for FR-language discovery.

Why:

- French-language query overlap
- audience often includes Algerian, Moroccan, and Tunisian communities
- community resource pages and prayer sections are common

### 3. Prayer-time and mosque directories

Useful if editorial inclusion is possible.

Why:

- direct topical overlap
- can reinforce city relevance

Risk:

- many directory sites are low quality
- only pursue if the site is maintained and clearly useful

### 4. Student Muslim associations

Good secondary layer.

Why:

- real communities
- often share Muslim resources, Ramadan links, prayer-time tools, and qibla tools

### 5. Official or institutional prayer-time sources

Primarily trust/citation targets, not classic backlink outreach targets.

Why:

- useful for method validation, citation, and product trust
- helps explain local timetable differences

## Outreach Angles

Use one of these. Do not send the same message everywhere.

### Angle 1: Community resource

Good for mosque and association websites.

Pitch:

- fast city prayer time lookup
- French and Arabic support
- Algeria city coverage
- next-prayer countdown
- browser-based, no app required

### Angle 2: Ramadan / prayer tools

Good for seasonal pages and resource lists.

Pitch:

- useful for visitors who want quick daily times by city
- supports Oran, Alger, Annaba, Bouira, Ain Benian

### Angle 3: Feedback-first soft outreach

Good when asking for a link directly would be too aggressive.

Pitch:

- ask them to test the page
- ask whether it is useful for their community
- only then suggest listing it in resources if they find it valuable

## Natural Anchor Examples

Use varied anchors. Do not over-repeat exact-match anchors.

French:

- horaires de prière à Oran
- horaires de prière en Algérie
- prochaine prière à Alger
- heure du fajr à Oran
- outil de priere par ville

Arabic:

- مواقيت الصلاة في وهران
- مواقيت الصلاة في الجزائر
- الصلاة القادمة في الجزائر العاصمة
- وقت الفجر في وهران

English:

- prayer times in Oran
- Algeria prayer times
- next prayer in Algiers

Brand/light brand:

- Adantimer prayer times
- Adantimer Algeria prayer pages

## Landing Page Mapping

Do not point everything to the homepage.

Use this mapping:

- Oran-focused mention -> `/fr/oran` or `/ar/oran`
- Algiers-focused mention -> `/fr/alger` or `/ar/alger`
- city prayer tools list -> `/fr/prayer-times` or `/ar/prayer-times`
- next prayer angle -> `/fr/next-prayer/alger` or `/ar/next-prayer/alger`

## Weekly Workflow

### Week 1

- build a shortlist of 20 to 30 real prospects
- keep only sites that are active and relevant
- send 5 to 8 highly tailored messages

### Week 2

- follow up once on non-responders
- record responses
- note which pages/anchors were accepted

### Week 3

- add another 5 to 8 prospects
- prioritize association and mosque sites over generic directories

## Prospect Qualification Checklist

A prospect should pass most of these:

- real organization or real community
- active site
- visible prayer or Islamic content
- clear contact route
- no casino / pills / random outbound spam
- content quality is acceptable
- audience overlaps with Algeria, North Africa, or FR/AR Muslim users

## Tracking Template

Track each prospect with:

- site
- URL
- target type
- language
- contact method
- contact person
- date first contacted
- follow-up date
- result
- linked page
- anchor used

## Practical Recommendation

For this phase, one relevant mosque or community link is worth more than many weak links.

The target is not "many backlinks."

The target is:

- a few clean FR/AR topical links
- pointed at Algeria priority pages
- from real Muslim community contexts
